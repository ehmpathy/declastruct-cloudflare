# research: domain terms

research on terms to coin for domain objects required to fulfill the wish, vision, and criteria.

---

## objective

maximize specificity + maximize intuition = ubiquitous language

---

## terminology from cloudflare

### zone

> "A DNS zone is a specific portion of the DNS namespace... which a specific organization or administrator manages."

source: [1] https://www.cloudflare.com/learning/dns/glossary/dns-zone/

> "The term DNS zone was coined by the Internet Engineering Task Force (IETF) in 1987."

source: [2] https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DNS_zone

### domain vs zone

> "The term domain is used in the business functions of the entity assigned to it, and the term zone is usually used for the configuration of DNS services."

source: [3] https://simpledns.plus/docs/definition-domains-vs-zones

### registrar terminology

> "A domain name registry is an organization that manages top-level domain names."

> "The registrar is an accredited organization... that sells domain names to the public."

> "A registrant is the person or company who registers a domain name."

source: [4] https://www.godaddy.com/help/what-is-the-difference-between-a-registry-registrar-and-registrant-8039

## term analysis

### zone

| term | source | pros | cons |
|------|--------|------|------|
| zone | cloudflare, RFC 1035 | industry standard, precise | technical |
| domain | common usage | intuitive | overloaded |
| site | web terminology | user-friendly | imprecise |

**decision**: keep "zone" — RFC standard, precise, cloudflare native.

prefix: `DomainZone` — clarifies this is a zone for a domain (not firewall zone, time zone).

### dns record

| term | source | pros | cons |
|------|--------|------|------|
| record | RFC 1035 | industry standard | generic |
| entry | common usage | intuitive | less precise |
| resource record | RFC 1035 | precise | verbose |

**decision**: keep "DnsRecord" — industry standard, clear.

prefix: `DomainDnsRecord` — clarifies context.

### registration

| term | source | pros | cons |
|------|--------|------|------|
| registration | cloudflare, ICANN | industry standard | long |
| domain | common usage | short | overloaded |
| deed | real estate | evocative | unfamiliar in tech |

**decision**: keep "Registration" — industry standard, maps to registrar concept.

prefix: `DomainRegistration` — clarifies this is domain ownership.

## final names

### treestruct

```
Declared                    # prefix: declared state (declastruct pattern)
└─ Cloudflare               # provider namespace
   └─ Domain                # domain context (DNS, registrar)
      ├─ Zone               # entity: DNS zone container
      ├─ DnsRecord          # entity: individual DNS record
      └─ Registration       # entity: registrar-level ownership
```

### domain objects

| object | name | rationale |
|--------|------|-----------|
| zone | `DeclaredCloudflareDomainZone` | RFC standard, cloudflare native |
| dns record | `DeclaredCloudflareDomainDnsRecord` | RFC standard, explicit context |
| registration | `DeclaredCloudflareDomainRegistration` | industry standard, clear intent |

### pattern

all names follow: `Declared{Provider}{Context}{Concept}`

---

## rejected alternatives

| rejected | reason |
|----------|--------|
| `CloudflareSite` | imprecise, web-centric |
| `CloudflareDomain` | overloaded (zone vs registration) |
| `DomainDeed` | unfamiliar, breaks industry convention |
| `DnsZone` | redundant (zone is already DNS concept) |
| `DomainEntry` | less precise than DnsRecord |

---

## summary

| object | final name |
|--------|------------|
| zone | DeclaredCloudflareDomainZone |
| dns record | DeclaredCloudflareDomainDnsRecord |
| registration | DeclaredCloudflareDomainRegistration |

terms maximize specificity (RFC-based) and intuition (clear name pattern).
